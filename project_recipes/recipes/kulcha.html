Kulcha Recipe

The classic Indian flatbread Kulcha is best enjoyed with a robust Punjabi Chana Masala. This combination was, is and will always remain a winner! So, in this post, I have got my Kulcha Bread for you to try at home.
It is a fact that Amritsar in Punjab houses the best kulchas in the world. But this Kulcha recipe of mine can also give a tough competition to them!

Ingredients

  • 2.5 cups all-purpose flour
  • 4 tablespoons curd
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 3/4 cup water
  • 2 to 2.5 teaspoons sugar
  • 3/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2.5 tablespoons oil
  • some ghee pr butter for roasting
  • extra flour
  • 1 to 2 tablespoons sesame seeds

Steps

  1. Mix the all-purpose flour, salt, sugar, baking powder and baking soda in a bowl. Make a well in the center. Add the yogurt, oil and water.

  2. Begin to mix the flour with the liquids first and then start to knead. Make a smooth, soft and supple dough.

  3. In the same bowl or pan, cover the dough with a wet kitchen napkin and let the dough rest for 2 hours.

  4. Make small balls of the dough and flatten them a bit. Sprinkle some nigella seeds or sesame seeds on the dough ball.

  5. With the rolling pin, flatten the dough and roll each into a small-sized kulcha.
    Do not roll thin but have a medium thickness similar to a paratha or slightly thicker than paratha.

  6. Heat a flat skillet or a tawa. Place the kulcha on the hot tawa. Cook one side partially till its 1/4th cooked. Flip and cook the other side till it is 1/2 cooked.

  7. Apply oil or ghee on both sides of kulcha while roasting. You can skip the oil or ghee if you prefer. Flip a few times as needed and roast the kulcha till it gets golden spots and is evenly cooked. Prepare more kulchas in a similar way.

  8. As you prepare them, stack the cooked kulcha in a roti-basket, so that they stay warm. Serve Kulcha hot with chana masala.
